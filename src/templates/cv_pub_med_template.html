Scholars@Duke Users: How to Complete the APT CV

  • The School of Medicine APT CV contains output from your Scholars@Duke profile displayed in a Word document that you can edit directly.

  • To make permanent changes, you must add or correct information in the source systems, either Scholars@Duke, Elements, dFac (Duke’s faculty system) or SPS (Duke’s Sponsored Projects System).

  • Titles, affiliations, and educational information comes from dFac, Duke’s faculty system. To add or correct information in dFac, contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

  • For more information, refer to Using the Scholars APT CV.

  • Delete this cover page before submitting your CV.

Correcting and editing source information

Name and Degrees:

Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

Primary Academic Appointment:

Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

Secondary Academic Appointment:

Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

Rank and date of first Duke Faculty Appointment:

Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

Medical License Number and Date:

Add in Scholars@Duke in the "Medical Licensure" section.

Education:

Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

Professional Training and Academic Career:

Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

Specialty Certifications and Dates:

Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

Duke University Faculty Appointments:

Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

Publications:

Add or edit in Elements, the publications system. Click the Help button to learn more about how to use Elements to add, edit, or claim your publications.
Publications from your Scholars@Duke profile will appear (or not appear) on this CV based on how the record (from PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, etc.) was tagged at the source. For a full explanation of how publications are categorized on the SoM CV, see https://about.scholars.duke.edu/medicinecv. Note that the categorization rules are determined by the Duke School of Medicine APT Committee and any changes would need to be approved by that committee.

Advisory and Consultant Appointments:

Add to your Scholars@Duke profile in the Service to the Profession section using service type "Consulting".

Scholarly Societies:

Add in Scholars@Duke in the Awards and Honors section using award type "Scholarly Society".

Professional Awards and Special Recognitions:

Add in Scholars@Duke in the Awards and Honors section

Scientific Reviewer:

Add in Scholars@Duke in the Service to the Profession section using service type "Editorial Activities".

Professional Societies, Service Organizations:

Add in Scholars@Duke in the Service to the Profession section.

Research Support:

Grants data comes from SPS and must be corrected on your behalf by your organization’s Scholars power user. Supplement this information using the "Fellowships, Supported Research & Other Grants" section in Scholars@Duke. You’ll need to add Grants Direct Costs and Grants Percent Effort directly to the generated CV Word document.

Mentoring Activities:

Add in Scholars@Duke in the Advising and Mentoring section

Education / Teaching Activities:

Add in Scholars@Duke in the Teaching Activities section

Invited Lectures and Presentations:

Add in Scholars@Duke in the Presentations and Appearances section using the following subtypes:

  • Named Lectures: Use type Keynote/Named Lecture

  • Visiting Professorships: Use type Visiting Professorship Lecture

  • International Meetings (Invited): Use service type "International Meeting or Conference". Do not include abstract presentations, industry sponsored symposiums, etc.

  • National Scientific Meetings (Invited): Use service type "National Scientific Meeting". Do not include abstract presentations, industry sponsored symposiums, etc.

  • Instructional Courses, workshops, symposiums (National): Use service type "Instructional Course, Workshop, or Symposium"

Clinical Activities:

Add in Scholars@Duke in the Clinical Activities section

Academic, Leadership, and Administrative Activities:

Add in Scholars@Duke in the Academic & Administrative Activities section. Scholars@Duke does not distinguish between School of Medicine and Hospital/DUHS activities, so you may need to move entries under the appropriate section in the exported CV Word document.
If you have questions or need help, please contact your department’s Scholars power user to submit a Help ticket.
