Scholars@Duke Users: How to Complete the APT CV

  • The School of Medicine APT CV contains output from your Scholars@Duke profile displayed in a Word document that you can edit directly.

  • To make permanent changes, you must add or correct information in the source systems, either Scholars@Duke, Elements, dFac (Duke’s faculty system) or SPS (Duke’s Sponsored Projects System).

  • Titles, affiliations, and educational information comes from dFac, Duke’s faculty system. To add or correct information in dFac, contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

  • For more information, refer to Using the Scholars APT CV.

  • Delete this cover page before submitting your CV.

Publications:

Publications from your Scholars@Duke profile will appear (or not appear) on this CV based on how the record (from PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, etc.) was tagged at the source. For example a journal article tagged as "Multicenter Study" would be listed under the section "Study-related manuscripts on multicenter clinical trials". For a full explanation of how publications are categorized on the SoM CV, see https://about.scholars.duke.edu/medicinecv. Note that the categorization rules are determined by the Duke School of Medicine APT Committee and any changes would need to be approved by them.

  • Refereed journal article: Journal Article type with either the Academic Article subtype or no subtype

  • Study-related manuscripts on multicenter clinical trials: Journal Article type, Clinical Trials subtype, and the Contributor role

  • Letters: Any publication type with subtype Letter

  • Editorials: Any publication type with subtype Editorial Comment

  • Books: Book type

  • Chapters: Book Section type

  • Selected abstracts: Journal Article type and subtype Abstract

  • Scientific reviews: Journal Article type and subtype Review

  • Non-authored publications: Any publication type with the Contributor role with the Author role removed

  • Other publications: Other Articles type and/or any of the subtypes not listed above
