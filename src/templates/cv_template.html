About the Scholars@Duke CV Export

  • Named Lectures: Use type Keynote/Named Lecture

  • The CV contains output from your Scholars@Duke profile displayed in a Word document that you can edit directly.

  • The Scholars@Duke CV is provided for convenience and might not include all of the information you wish to include on your CV.
  • If you wish to make changes to the data in your CV, it is recommended that, where possible, you update the data in the source system, either Scholars@Duke, Elements, dFac (Duke’s faculty system) or SPS (Duke’s Sponsored Projects System). Once the source data is up-to-date, you can regenerate your CV with the updated data.
  • Titles, affiliations, and educational information comes from dFac, Duke’s faculty system. To add or correct information in dFac, contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).

Delete this cover page before submitting your CV.

Correcting and editing source information**
Name and Degrees:
Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).
Current Appointments and Affiliations:
Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).
Education, Training and Certifications:
Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).
Duke Appointment History:
Must be updated in dFac on your behalf; contact your organization's Scholars power user(s).
Other Academic Positions:
Add or edit data in Scholars@Duke in the Academic Positions Outside Duke section.
Publications:
Add or edit in Elements, the publications system. Click the Help button to learn more about how to use Elements to add, edit, or claim your publications.
Awards and Honors:
Add in Scholars@Duke in the Awards and Honors section
Grants, Fellowships and Supported Research:
Grants data comes from the SPS system and must be corrected on your behalf by your organization’s Scholars power user. Supplement this information using the "Fellowships, Supported Research & Other Grants" section in Scholars@Duke.
Mentoring Activities:
Add or edit in Scholars@Duke in the Advising & Mentoring section.
Courses Taught:
Courses data comes from PeopleSoft.
Scholarly, Clinical & Service Activities:**
Add or edit directly in Scholars@Duke. There are subsections for Presentations and Appearances, Outreach, Service to the Profession, Service to Duke, Academic and Administrative Activities, and Clinical Activities.
